🕵️01What is Multiplayer Mode?

Mystery-o-Matic Multiplayer turns the daily puzzle into a collaborative investigation. Instead of one player reading all the clues, each player receives their own exclusive slice of the evidence. You must talk to each other, compare notes, and piece the mystery together as a team.

Everyone is solving the same daily mystery — same victim, same locations, same suspects, same weapons. The only difference is that no single player can crack it alone. The clue deck is divided between players, and cooperation is the only path to the answer.

🎭 Solo vs Multiplayer
The puzzle is identical whether you play alone or with friends. Multiplayer splits the clue load and adds a shared investigation layer — the deduction logic stays the same. If you already know how to play Solo, the only new concepts are saving, sharing, and merging grids.
1
Host creates a room & shares the code
The host picks the player count, generates a room code, and sends it to everyone.
2
Everyone joins using the code
Each player enters their display name and the room code to enter the lobby.
3

Clues are automatically split. You only see the clues assigned to your slot.

Work through your clues and mark your deductions. No one can see your grid yet.
5

Saving makes your grid visible when a teammate opens your named journal tab.
6
🔗 Share Grid to contribute to the team merge
When ready to collaborate, push your confident marks into the shared journal for everyone to merge.
7
Refresh Shared Journal, resolve conflicts, accuse!
Merge everyone's shared marks, discuss conflicts, then make your accusation.
🏠02Setting Up a Room (Host)

The host is the player who creates the room. Switch to Multiplayer mode using the toggle at the top of the page. The setup card appears with two options — create or join. As the host, use the create section.

1
Enter your display name
This is the name other players will see in the lobby chips and journal tabs. Pick something short and recognisable.
2
Select the number of players
Choose 2, 3, or 4. This determines how clues are divided and how many journal tabs appear. You cannot change this after generating the code. Agree with your group first!
3
Click "Generate Room Code →"
A unique room code appears. Copy and share it with everyone who needs to join.
⚠️ Player count is locked after generation
Once you generate the room code, the player count is fixed. If you need a different number, refresh the page to reset and generate a new code. Make sure everyone agrees before generating!
🚪03Joining a Room

Everyone who is not the host uses the Join section of the setup card. You need the room code the host shared with you.

1
Enter your display name
Use any name you like — but remember it. You'll need the exact same name to reconnect if you refresh or switch devices.
2
Enter the room code
Paste the code the host sent you. It is not case-sensitive.
3
Click "Join Room →"
You'll be placed in the next available slot and the lobby will load automatically.
💡 Rejoining from another device?
Enter the same room code + same display name in the Join section. The game recognises you by this combination and restores your slot and any server-saved progress automatically.
👥04The Players Panel (Lobby)

Once you create or join a room, a players panel appears at the top of the page. This is your lobby at a glance — it shows who is in, their display names, and the room code.

    Other players show their real display names once they've joined. Empty slots appear as grey placeholders until someone joins.

The game sections — clues, journal, notes, and accusation — all unlock as soon as you enter the lobby. You do not need to wait for all players before starting to read your clues.

🔍05Your Clues — Reading & Sharing

This is the heart of the multiplayer experience. The clue deck is automatically divided between all players — no setup needed. Each clue belongs to exactly one player based on a round-robin split.

📋 How clues are split
Clues are assigned round-robin: clue 1 → player 1, clue 2 → player 2, clue 3 → player 3, clue 4 → player 1 again, and so on. The split is deterministic — everyone in the same room on the same day gets the same assignment. Your clue navigator only shows the clues assigned to you.

Use the ← Previous Clue and Next Clue → buttons to navigate through your assigned clues. You will never see clues assigned to other players — you have to communicate!

💬 You must communicate!
No single player has enough clues to solve the mystery alone. Read your clues aloud to your group, share in a chat, or take turns calling out what you found. The initial puzzle setup (who was where at the final time, which weapons exist) is visible to all players — only the investigation clues are split.

💾07Save — Your Working Copy for Teammates

Clicking 💾 Save stores your current grid marks to your browser's local storage and to your private server copy (my_grid_marks). This is your working copy — the version of your grid that teammates will see when they open your named journal tab (📓 Alice, 📓 Bob, etc.) and click 🔄 Load [Your Name]'s Grid.

Think of Save as saying: "Here is my current progress — teammates who want to follow along can load my tab and see where I am." This includes all your marks — confirmed ✓, eliminated ✗, and even uncertain ? ones — because a teammate loading your tab is specifically interested in your personal working state, not just your final confident conclusions.

💡 Save early, save often
Save protects your work if the tab refreshes or closes unexpectedly. It also keeps your named journal tab up to date for teammates who want to peek at your progress. Make it a habit to save after every few deductions — it costs nothing and protects everything.
⚠️ Save ≠ Share Grid
Saving does not push your marks into the collaborative Shared Journal merge. For that, you need 🔗 Share Grid (next section). The two actions serve two different audiences — your named tab readers (Save) vs the team merge (Share Grid).
🔗08Share Grid — Contributing to the Team Merge

Clicking 🔗 Share Grid uploads your current marks to the shared grid server (shared_grid_marks). These marks feed directly into the 📘 Shared Journal tab, where all players' contributions are merged and conflicts are highlighted.

Think of Share Grid as saying: "I am confident enough in these deductions to put them into the team's combined picture." Unlike Save, Share Grid does not update your named journal tab — it targets the collaborative merge specifically.

ActionUpdates your named tab (📓)?Updates Shared Journal (📘)?
💾 Save✓ Yes — teammates can Load your tab✗ No
🔗 Share Grid✗ No✓ Yes — feeds the team merge
🎯 What to put in the shared merge
Share your confident deductions — cells you've confirmed ✓ or definitively ruled out ✗. Hold back uncertain ? marks that you're still processing. The shared merge works best when everyone contributes only well-founded conclusions, keeping conflict noise low.

You can share your grid multiple times. Each time you click Share Grid, your previous shared marks are replaced with your current state. So refine your deductions over time and re-share as your confidence grows — the shared journal always reflects your latest push.

📘10The Shared Journal — Merging & Conflicts

The 📘 Shared Journal tab is your team's combined deduction board. It merges all players' shared marks (pushed via 🔗 Share Grid) into a single grid, highlights disagreements, and gives you a conflict log to resolve together.

Click 🔄 Refresh Shared Grid to pull the latest marks from all players and rebuild the merged view. Do this whenever a teammate announces they've shared their grid.

How merging works: For each cell, the mark from the player who shared most recently wins. If two players have different marks for the same cell, it is flagged as a conflict — shown with a yellow ⚡ highlight in the grid and listed in the Conflict Log below.

The ⚡ Conflict Log lists every disagreement in the format:
📍 Location → Person → Time — PlayerA: ✓ vs PlayerB: ✗

Conflicts are not errors — they are discussion points. Talk through each one with your team and figure out whose deduction is supported by the evidence. Once one side is convinced and updates their grid, they click 🔗 Share Grid again with the corrected mark. When everyone is in agreement and pushes updated shared grids, the conflict flag disappears automatically the next time anyone refreshes the Shared Journal — because the two players now agree on that cell.

⚠️ Refresh does not auto-update
The Shared Journal does not live-sync. You must click 🔄 Refresh Shared Grid manually each time you want to pull the latest state. When a teammate shares their grid, they should let the group know so everyone can refresh and see the updated merge.
📝11Notes — Private & Shared

The Notes section works similarly to the journal, but for free-form text. There are two tabs: 📓 My Notes and 📘 Shared Notes.

📓 My Notes 📘 Shared Notes
Only visible to you
💾 Save vs 🔗 Share Note — Notes Edition
💾 Save stores your note in your browser's local storage only — completely private, just for you. Teammates cannot see your saved note at all.

🔗 Share Note pushes your current note text to the Shared Notes feed. Unlike the grid, notes accumulate — each push adds a new entry rather than replacing old ones. This creates a running log of everyone's observations over the course of the game.

The 📘 Shared Notes tab shows all pushed notes in chronological order, newest at the bottom — like a team investigation log. Each entry is labelled with the player's name and the time they shared it. Click 🔄 Refresh to load the latest entries.

📓 My Notes 📘 Shared Notes
All players' pushed notes
💡 Use Shared Notes as your team's reasoning trail
Push a note whenever you have a key insight or want to flag something for the team. Since notes accumulate rather than overwrite, the feed becomes a record of your group's evolving theory — useful to scroll back through when resolving conflicts or making your final accusation.
⚖️12Making Your Accusation

Once you have enough evidence, use the Accusation section at the bottom of the page. Select the suspect, time of murder, and weapon, then click Accuse!

The answer is verified against a cryptographic hash — wrong answers are penalised by reducing your final score rank on the Sleuth-o-Meter™, but you can keep trying. Each wrong guess costs you a point, so use your deductions carefully before committing.

🎯 Team accusation strategy
In multiplayer, each player accuses independently — there is no shared accusation screen. For the best team experience, align your final deductions in the Shared Notes before anyone accuses, so you can go in together with the same answer.
🔄13Session Persistence & Reconnecting

Sessions are tied to today's date. If you reload the page or come back later the same day, the game automatically detects your saved session and drops you back into the lobby — no need to re-enter your name or room code.

📅 New day = fresh start
Each day is a new puzzle with a new room. Your session from yesterday will not carry over. The game detects this automatically and clears old session data.

To reconnect from a different device mid-session, go to the Join section and enter your original display name and room code. The server recognises you and restores your slot. Your grid marks will be restored from your most recent server save.

✨14Quick Tips for a Smooth Game
  • 🔑
    Agree on player count before the host generates the code. It cannot be changed after. A quick message to your group before starting saves a lot of confusion.
  • 💬
    Read your clues aloud — don't just share grid marks, narrate what you found. Hearing the full clue text helps everyone reason together.
  • 💾
    Save your grid often. It protects your work from browser refreshes and keeps your named journal tab up to date for teammates who want to follow your progress.
  • 🔗
    Share Grid when you have confident deductions. Your uncertain ? marks are great for your own thinking but can generate unnecessary conflicts in the shared merge. Share your ✓ and ✗ marks when you're ready for team input.
  • 🔄
    Refresh Shared Grid whenever a teammate announces they've shared. The shared journal does not auto-update — it's manual so you control when you pull new information.
  • ⚡
    Treat conflict log entries as conversation starters. A conflict means two players disagree — that's useful! Talk through each one. Once the disagreeing player is convinced and re-shares with the corrected mark, the conflict flag disappears on the next refresh.
  • 📝
    Use Shared Notes to build a running theory. Push a note whenever you have a key insight. The chronological feed builds a record of your team's reasoning that everyone can scroll back through.
  • 🕐
    Same room code + same display name = same session. Use this to reconnect from another device or a fresh browser tab mid-game.
  • 🧩
    The shared journal is a collaboration tool, not the final answer. Use it to consolidate team knowledge, but keep your private journal for deductions you're still working through.
